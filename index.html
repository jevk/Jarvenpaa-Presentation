Järvenpää

Järvenpää is a small town of 44 thousand in the Uusimaa region, Finland, about 37 kilometers from the capital city, Helsinki. It is surrounded by lakes and forests, contributing to its picturesque natural scenery.

History

The history of Järvenpää dates back to the late 19th century when it was established as a railway station. It experienced significant growth during the early 20th century, especially with the arrival of the renowned Finnish composer Jean Sibelius, who lived in Järvenpää for a considerable portion of his life.

Railway Station

The Järvenpää railway station was established in 1858 as part of Finland's expanding railway network during the late 19th century. Its strategic location played a crucial role in connecting the town to other major cities and regions, facilitating trade, transportation, and communication. It is also one of the oldest railway stations in the entire country.

Growth

Järvenpää is one of the most fastest growing towns in Finland by far. By the end of 2017, Järvenpää's population was 42 thousand, of which 41 thousand lives within urban areas. The population growth in 2017 was 2.5% a year. It is estimated that by the year 2040, Järvenpää's population would be over 52 thousand.


Infrastructure

The infrastructure of Järvenpää is good enough to not own a car. You can get around with buses and the downtown Järvenpää is very walkable. Trains also go to Helsinki and Riihimäki every 30 minutes, which makes transportation to the capital extremely easy. The town is also very bicycle friendly where multiple places have bike lanes and you are allowed to ride a bike on most pedestrian roads.

Healthcare

Public healthcare in Järvenpää all functions in the JUST (Järvenpään Uusi Sosiaali- ja Terveyskeskus (english: New Social and Healthcare Centre of Järvenpää)). Its purpose was to centralize all healthcare in the town to one convenient place, even dental healthcare. Although most of the public sector functions in the JUST there's another HUS (Helsingin ja Uudenmaan Sairaanhoitopiiri) location (HUS is the centralized public healthcare sector in Uusimaa.) In addition to that, there are private healthcare centers as well, most of these in the downtown Järvenpää.
